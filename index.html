FAQ

Your questions, answered.

Everything you need to know before we start.

How long does a branding project take?

+

Most brand identity projects run between 2 and 4 weeks from kick-off to final delivery. A focused logo and guidelines package can be completed in 2 weeks. A full brand system including motion design and web design takes 4 to 6 weeks. Every project starts with a clear timeline so you always know where we are.

What does a brand identity project cost?

+

Brand identity projects vary in scope and investment. At Unpeel, projects start from around €1.000 for a focused logo and brand foundation. A full brand system — including strategy, guidelines, motion and web — typically sits between €1.000 and €3.000+. Every project receives a clear, itemised quote upfront. No hidden costs, no surprises.

What is the difference between a logo and a brand identity?

+

A logo is a single mark. A brand identity is the full visual and verbal system around it — typography, colour, tone of voice, imagery style and usage guidelines. The logo is one piece; the identity is what makes your brand recognisable and consistent everywhere it appears.

When does a startup need professional branding?

+

Earlier than most founders think. A strong identity from the start sets the tone for everything — your website, your pitch deck, your packaging, your social presence. Getting it right early means you build on a solid foundation instead of retrofitting credibility later. That said, a focused logo package can be a smart starting point if budget is tight.

What do I receive at the end of a brand identity project?

+

All brand files in every format you need (AI, PDF, PNG, SVG), a brand guidelines document, and support for applying the identity to print, web, social and packaging.

What types of clients do you work with?

+

Unpeel works with startups, restaurants and hospitality businesses, product-based brands, hotels, artists and creative founders — both new brands and rebrands. The common thread is founders and businesses who want design that is honest, strategic and built to last. If you are building something from scratch or feel your current identity no longer reflects where you are going, we are probably a good fit.

Do you work with clients outside Ghent or Belgium?

+

Yes. I work remotely with clients across Europe. Everything happens over video call and shared tools — location is never a barrier.

What makes a strong brand identity?

+

Clarity, consistency and character. A strong identity knows what it stands for, looks the same everywhere, and has a distinct personality that people recognise and remember. It starts with strategy — understanding your purpose, your audience and your positioning — and ends with a visual system that expresses all of that without explanation.
